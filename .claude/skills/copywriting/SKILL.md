## Voice & Tone Rules

### The Bakes by Coral Voice

Coralyn IS the brand. Every piece of copy should sound like it comes from a warm, passionate baker who genuinely loves what she does — not from a marketing department.

**Always:**
- Write in **first person singular** ("I", "my", "me") — never "we" or "our"
- Sound like a real person talking to a friend
- Lead with emotion and celebration, then details
- Make gluten-free feel exciting, not limiting
- Reference Cincinnati and local community when natural

**Never:**
- Use corporate or clinical language
- Make it sound like a chain bakery
- Lead with restrictions ("free from," "no gluten")
- Use pressure tactics or false urgency
- Over-promise on delivery/availability (pickup only, lead times matter)

### Tone Spectrum

| Context                | Tone Dial                          |
|------------------------|------------------------------------|
| Product descriptions   | Warm + indulgent (sensory words)   |
| Order forms            | Clear + reassuring                 |
| Social media           | Fun + personal + celebratory       |
| Wedding inquiries      | Elegant + personal + attentive     |
| FAQs / policies        | Friendly + direct + transparent    |
| Email confirmations    | Grateful + helpful                 |
| Blog posts             | Conversational + knowledgeable     |
| Error messages         | Empathetic + solution-oriented     |

## Copy Formulas & Templates

### Headlines

**Formula 1 — Benefit + Qualifier:**
> "Gluten-Free [Product] That Tastes Like the Real Thing"

**Formula 2 — Emotion + Product:**
> "Celebrate With Custom [Cakes/Cookies] Made Just for You"

**Formula 3 — Challenge + Solution:**
> "Tired of GF Baked Goods That Taste Like Cardboard? Same. That's Why I Started Baking."

**Formula 4 — Personal + Invitation:**
> "I'd Love to Bake Something Special for Your [Occasion]"

### Product Descriptions

**Template — Sensory-first:**
```
[Sensory hook — what it tastes/looks/feels like].
[What makes it special — ingredient or technique detail].
[Perfect-for statement — occasion or pairing].
[Trust signal — GF kitchen, made from scratch].
```

**Example — Chocolate Chip Cookies:**
> Warm, gooey centers with crispy golden edges and a delicate sea salt finish. I use real butter and premium chocolate chips — baked fresh in my dedicated gluten-free kitchen. Perfect for cookie trays, gifts, or just because you deserve something amazing. You'd never guess they're gluten-free.

### CTAs (Call-to-Action Hierarchy)

| Priority | CTA Text                        | Use Case                    |
|----------|---------------------------------|-----------------------------|
| Primary  | "Order Now"                     | Direct purchase pages       |
| Primary  | "Start Your Order"              | Order hub, product pages    |
| Primary  | "Get a Custom Quote"            | Cakes, weddings, large orders|
| Secondary| "See the Menu"                  | Homepage, about page        |
| Secondary| "View My Work"                  | Gallery links               |
| Secondary| "Book a Tasting"                | Wedding funnel              |
| Tertiary | "Learn More"                    | Blog, FAQ links             |
| Tertiary | "Get in Touch"                  | Contact page links          |
| Tertiary | "Follow Along"                  | Social media links          |

### CTA Writing Rules

1. Use action verbs — "Start," "Order," "Book," "Get," "See"
2. Keep it to 2–4 words when possible
3. First-person framing for invitations: "I'd love to hear from you" (not a button, but supporting text)
4. Never use "Submit" on forms — use "Send My Inquiry," "Place My Order," "Request a Quote"
5. Pair CTAs with a reassuring micro-copy line: "No commitment — just tell me about your event"


### Product Page (Generic Template)

```
[Page Title — Product Name]

[Hero description — 1-2 sentences, sensory + emotional]

[Key details grid: Price | Serves | Lead Time | Flavors]

[How it works — 3 steps:
 1. Choose your [flavors/design/size]
 2. Pick your pickup date
 3. I'll bake it fresh for you]

[Trust section: "Everything I bake is 100% gluten-free,
made from scratch in my dedicated home kitchen."]

[CTA: Start Your Order]
```

```
[Personal photo + warm greeting]

Hi, I'm Coralyn — [personal story hook].

[Origin story — why GF baking, personal connection]

[What makes the bakery different — dedicated kitchen, quality]

[What drives her — celebration, making people happy]

[Cincinnati connection — community, local love]

[Invitation to order — warm CTA]
```

## Words & Phrases to Avoid

| Avoid                    | Use Instead                              |
|--------------------------|------------------------------------------|
| gluten-free alternative  | gluten-free [product name]               |
| substitute               | the real thing / classic                 |
| diet / dietary           | lifestyle, preference, needs             |
| restriction              | (just don't mention it)                  |
| facility / plant         | my kitchen, my home bakery               |
| processed                | handcrafted, from scratch                |
| cheap / discount         | value, savings, special pricing          |
| order now / hurry        | I'd love to bake for you                 |
| we / our / the team      | I / my / me                              |
| click here               | [descriptive link text]                  |
| submit                   | send, place, request, book               |

## Copy Review Checklist

When writing or reviewing any copy for Bakes by Coral, verify:

- [ ] Written in first person ("I" / "my")?
- [ ] Warm and personal tone (not corporate)?
- [ ] Gluten-free positioned as a positive, not a limitation?
- [ ] Sensory language for food descriptions?
- [ ] Accurate pricing and lead times (check product-marketing-context)?
- [ ] CTA is action-oriented and not "Submit"?
- [ ] Cincinnati / local references where appropriate?
- [ ] No pressure tactics or false urgency?
- [ ] Consistent with brand color and typography references?
- [ ] Pickup-only model clearly communicated (no delivery promises)?
