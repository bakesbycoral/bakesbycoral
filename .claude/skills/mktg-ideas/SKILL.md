## Local Cincinnati Marketing Tactics

### Community Partnerships

1. **Cincinnati wedding vendors** — Cross-refer with photographers, florists, planners, venues
2. **Local coffee shops** — Offer GF cookies for their pastry case (wholesale or consignment)
3. **Cincinnati food bloggers** — Gift tasting boxes for reviews and features
4. **Celiac/GF support groups** — Sponsor events, offer group discounts, educational talks
5. **Farmers markets** — Weekend market presence when capacity allows
6. **Cincinnati events** — Taste of Cincinnati, local food festivals, bridal shows
7. **Local gyms/wellness studios** — Partner for "healthier treat" positioning
8. **Schools with celiac students** — Offer classroom-safe celebration treats

### Local PR & Media

- Pitch to Cincinnati Enquirer food section
- Local TV morning show segment ("Local GF Baker Breaks the Mold")
- Cincinnati Magazine "Best of" submissions
- CincyMagazine.com, UrbanCincy, local food blogs
- Podcast guest appearances (food, small business, celiac-focused)

### Google Business Profile Optimization

- Post weekly updates (new flavors, seasonal offerings, behind-the-scenes)
- Respond to every review within 24 hours
- Upload new photos monthly
- Use Google Q&A for common questions
- Track "gluten-free bakery Cincinnati" search visibility

## Growth Tactics

### Referral Program

- "Refer a friend, get $5 off your next order"
- Give the friend $5 off their first order too
- Track via unique coupon codes in the coupon system
- Promote in post-purchase emails and order confirmation pages

### Customer Reviews & Testimonials

- Ask for reviews in post-pickup follow-up email
- Feature testimonials on product pages
- Screenshot Instagram mentions and add to gallery
- Create a "Love Notes" highlight on Instagram

### Email List Growth

- Newsletter signup on website (pop-up or inline form)
- Offer incentive: "Join my email list for first access to new flavors"
- Add signup link to Instagram bio
- Collect emails at any in-person events
- Cross-promote on social: "My email subscribers heard about this first!"

### Content Marketing Flywheel

```
Blog Post → Social Media Posts → Newsletter Feature → Pinterest Pin
    ↑                                                        ↓
    ←←←←←←←←←←← Google Traffic ←←←←←←←←←←←←←←←←←←←←←←←←←←←
```

Each piece of content feeds the next channel, creating compound growth.

### Seasonal Pre-Order Strategy

1. Announce seasonal items 3–4 weeks before the holiday
2. Open pre-orders 2–3 weeks before
3. Send "last chance" email 1 week before the order deadline
4. Create urgency naturally: "I can only take [X] orders for [holiday]"
5. Share sold-out announcements to build demand for next time

## Marketing Psychology Principles

### Applied to Bakes by Coral

| Principle           | Application                                                    |
|---------------------|----------------------------------------------------------------|
| Social Proof        | Customer testimonials, order count ("500+ celebrations served") |
| Scarcity            | Limited pickup slots, seasonal flavors (genuinely limited)      |
| Reciprocity         | Free flavor guides, GF baking tips blog, helpful content        |
| Authority           | "100% dedicated GF kitchen," celiac-safe certification claims   |
| Liking              | Coralyn's personal story, warm brand voice, authentic photos    |
| Commitment          | Small first step (newsletter signup → tasting box → full order) |
| Loss Aversion       | "Don't miss spring flavors" (not "Buy now before it's too late")|
| Anchoring           | Show premium cake designs first, then standard feels like a deal|

### Ethical Boundaries

- Never create false scarcity — if something is limited, it's genuinely limited
- Never use countdown timers for fake urgency
- Never pressure customers — the brand voice is inviting, not pushy
- Always be transparent about pricing, lead times, and policies

## Budget-Friendly Marketing Ideas ($0–$100/month)

### Free ($0)

- Consistent social media posting (4–5x/week)
- Google Business Profile optimization and weekly posts
- Blog content creation (1–2 posts/month)
- Email newsletters to existing subscribers
- Engaging with local food accounts on Instagram
- Customer review requests in follow-up emails

- Cross-promotion with other local businesses

### Low Budget ($25–$100/month)

- Instagram post boosts for best-performing content ($5–$10/boost)
- Local Facebook group promotion
- Simple branded packaging/stickers for word-of-mouth
- Google Business Profile photo shoots (monthly with phone)
- Canva Pro for branded templates ($13/month)
- Seasonal mini photo shoots (DIY or trade with local photographer)
